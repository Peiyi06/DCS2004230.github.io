Chinese New Year

Chinese New Year is the Chinese festival that celebrates the beginning of a new year on the traditional Chinese calendar. The festival is commonly referred to as the Spring Festival in China as the spring season in the lunisolar calendar traditionally starts with lichun, the first of the twenty-four solar terms which the festival celebrates around the time of. Marking the end of winter and the beginning of the spring season, observances traditionally take place from New Year's Eve, the evening preceding the first day of the year to the Lantern Festival, held on the 15th day of the year. The first day of Chinese New Year begins on the new moon that appears between 21 January and 20 February. In 2021, the first day of the Chinese New Year will be on Friday, 12 February, which is the Year of the Ox

Hari Raya Aidiladha

Hari Raya Aidiladha, Hari Raya Korban or Hari Raya Haji is a festival celebrated by Muslims around the world. This festival is the largest festival in Islam besides Aidilfitri which is celebrated every 1 Syawal. Aidiladha is celebrated on the 10th, 11th, 12th and 13th day of the month of Zulhijjah every year. Starting with Takbir Aidiladha on the night of 10 Zulhijjah, followed by the Aidiladha sunat prayers and the Aidiladha sermon on the morning of 10 Zulhijjah, the celebration is followed by sacrificial worship that can be done either on 10, 11, 12 or during the day 13 Zulhijjah, all these times are called Hari Tasyrik.

Hari Deepavali

The celebration of Deepavali or Diwali is a festival among Hindus signifying victory by the power of good over the power of evil, symbolically addressed in the sense of light that illuminates the darkness. The implicit meaning is to instill positive values in ourselves to become better individuals by overcoming negative values in ourselves for spiritual development. Deepavali day begins with rubbing sesame oil on the head and body to cool the body, followed by a prayer ceremony with family members at home and blessings from parents and parents before going to the temple. Everyone will be wearing new clothes, sharing sweets specially prepared for Deepavali as well as visiting the homes of relatives and friends. At night, lights were lit and fireworks and fireworks enlivened the celebration.
